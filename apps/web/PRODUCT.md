## Users

OMNIPITCH serves a spectrum, not a single persona, and the surfaces map to it. The front door is a football fan watching a live WC26 match who wants skin in the game without gambling — second-screen or focused, wanting to act on their read of the moment. The depth end is a prediction-market or crypto-native trader fluent in Polymarket-style markets and on-chain settlement who wants a real live football market with a pro terminal. Between them sit live sports-trading enthusiasts who love in-play price movement. The Home and onboarding surfaces must stay legible to a mainstream first-timer who has never seen a live exchange; the Terminal earns the right to be dense for the trader who wants it. Whatever the user, the context is the same: a live match is running and they want to price a view and trade it in real time.

## Product Purpose

OMNIPITCH turns a live football match into a real-time, tradeable market. Prices move with the game via an LMSR pricing engine; users spend granted play-money credits (1000 per match), never cash — there are no deposits and no payouts, ever. Settlement is proven on-chain through TxLINE proofs verified by an on-chain Anchor program, with no admin result path. The product exists so fans and traders can express a live read as a price and act on it instantly, and trust that every outcome is provably settled rather than asserted. Success is a user reading a live moment, pricing it, and placing a trade in one gesture — coming back match after match because the market feels honest and the settlement is verifiable.

## Positioning

A live football exchange where every settlement is provable on-chain and no cash ever changes hands. The single claim every screen reinforces: trade the match in real time, play-money, provably settled — a game of skill, not a book.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, alive. The voice is plain verbs and sentence case: say price, trade, credits — never bet, stake, odds, or wager. The surface is a quiet dark room where everything holds still and legible; all visual boldness lives in one signature element, the Momentum River, which comes alive on live moments. Terminal-grade precision at rest, motion only when the game moves.

## Anti-references

Not a sportsbook or betting site — no neon casino felt, gold coins, flashing odds boards, or "BET NOW" urgency; the play-money, no-gambling invariant is load-bearing and the aesthetic must not borrow gambling's language or pressure. Not a crypto-casino or degen app — no gradient-drenched hype, no glassmorphism, no token-shill energy. Not a generic SaaS dashboard — no default card grids, hero-metric tiles, or indigo-on-white admin look. Not a cluttered, ad-stuffed live-scores portal where every pixel shouts. The design law's hard bans hold: no gradients, no glassmorphism, no light mode in v1, no lorem ipsum — real WC26 data everywhere.

## Design Principles

Quiet, except the River. One signature element carries all the visual energy so the live signal reads clearly; everything else stays still. Boldness is spent in one place, deliberately.

Provable, not promised. Every outcome settles on-chain and the interface shows the proof rather than asking for trust. There is no admin override to design around — the chain is the source of truth.

Play-money is the point, not a caveat. The product never adopts gambling's vocabulary, urgency, or reward mechanics. Credits, price, and trade are the whole world; the fun is being right, not cashing out.

Read the moment, act in one gesture. Every screen collapses the distance between seeing a live moment and trading on it. Latency between insight and action is the enemy.

Earned familiarity. Behave like the professional tools users already trust — exchange conventions, terminal depth, standard affordances — so the interface disappears into the task instead of inventing novelty for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text holds at least 4.5:1 contrast against the dark surface everywhere. Direction is never carried by color alone — the green/pink up/down pair is always reinforced with an arrow, sign, or position so the market reads for color-blind users. Every animation (the Momentum River, price flashes, reveals) ships a prefers-reduced-motion alternative, typically a crossfade or instant state change. All interactive surfaces — trade tickets, tabs, navigation — are fully keyboard-operable with visible focus states.
