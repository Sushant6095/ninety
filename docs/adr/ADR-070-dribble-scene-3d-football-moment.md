# ADR 070 The 3D football moment — a stylized dribbler scores, and the price ticks up

Status: accepted 2026-07-17 (`landing/dribble-scene` → `merge/live-integration`). Builds on ADR-069 (the long landing — this fills the deferred "3D wow" slot as section 2b), ADR-058 (3D legal on the landing hero/story, banned on live-price surfaces), ADR-053 (three sanctioned, lazy). House pattern: WorldGlobe (vanilla three, tokens via resolveColor, IO-gated rAF, DPR ≤ 1.5, full dispose).

Context: the landing needed its interactive "wow" — a scripted football story in 3D: a dribbler weaves past four defenders, beats the keeper, scores; the net ripples, the scene flashes green, and the market reprices. The spec offered two techniques: realistic (Mixamo rigged GLTF clips via drei useGLTF/useAnimations) or a sanctioned stylized fallback, with the explicit rule "a smooth stylized scene beats a stuttering realistic one — decide by MotionScore, not vibes."

Decision — **the stylized scene, on vanilla three, zero new deps.**
- **Technique:** `features/landing/DribbleScene.tsx` — abstract glowing figures (shared CapsuleGeometry bodies + sphere heads, unlit MeshBasicMaterial only, no lights) on a dark token pitch with up-green line markings. The run is a CatmullRomCurve3 weave (getPointAt/getTangentAt, preallocated scratch vectors — zero per-frame allocs); defenders lunge as the dribbler arrives and hold a wrong-footed "beaten" tilt once passed; the keeper dives the wrong way; the shot runs at 0.38× slow-mo with an up-token trail; the goal fires a net ripple (damped radial sine on a 9×6 line grid), an additive green flash, and a DOM price chip that ticks **41.0 → 61.4** with the token `.flash-up` grammar — the same repricing story the hero and the loop tell (read-out-loud coherent, no fourth match invented). ~7.6 s story loop, cinematic lerped camera (track the run → settle low for the shot).
- **Why not the realistic path (honest, two reasons):** (1) *asset-blocked* — Mixamo requires an interactive Adobe login; there is no headless download, and no clearly-licensed redistributable football-rig GLTF was found; (2) *not installed* — drei isn't a dep, and the stylized scene needs no loader, so vanilla three per the house WorldGlobe pattern beats adding R3F reconciler + drei for one scene. The fallback was explicitly sanctioned, fits the dark-terminal aesthetic, and measured cleaner than any rigged scene would.
- **Discipline:** landing-only (grep-proven: `three` importers are exactly `features/landing/*`); `next/dynamic ssr:false` (First Load JS of `/` unchanged at 259 kB); rAF only while IntersectionObserver-visible; DPR ≤ 1.5; full dispose (geometries, materials, texture, renderer); `prefers-reduced-motion` → the story is pre-run to the goal and rendered ONCE, no loop. No real-person likeness — an abstract #10, no name, no number, no kit branding.

Consequences / VERIFY (LOCAL PRODUCTION, :3100): build green (18 pages), tsc clean, **axe 0**. **WATCHED the motion** (6 captured frames across the loop): the dribble reads, beaten defenders tilt and fall behind, the keeper dives the wrong way, the ball beats him inside the far post, the chip flips 41.0 → **61.4** and resets with the loop. **FCP 88 ms** (hero untouched). **MotionScore by measurement: steady-state 0 long tasks across 12 s of the scene looping in view; full-scroll pass 553 ms/1 task ≈ the ADR-069 baseline (524/1)** — no Thrashing regression; the one-time 3D chunk parse is load-time, not run-time. Scene canvas 1695 px (DPR cap live). law-guard blocked the single hex fallback during authoring — removed; every color resolves from tokens.css. Verdict: `design/verdicts/2026-07-17-dribble-scene.md`; provenance row appended. Upgrade path if ever wanted: drop a licensed rigged GLTF in `public/` and swap the figures — the timeline/camera/goal machinery is reusable as-is.
